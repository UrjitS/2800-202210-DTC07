You are what you eat!

Your diet affects the state of your mind.

Benefits of Improving your DIET

Health
  • Studies show a healthy diet reduces the symptoms of mental illnesses
  • Highly processed foods aggravate, and increase the risk of developing symptoms
  • It is postulated that modest dietary changes can have an huge impact on reducing mental and neurological diseases

    • (If you'd like to learn more refer to: this study or this article.)

Brainpower
  • A balanced diet helps you think more quickly and clearly, and allows you to feel more alert.
  • It improves your concentration and attention span
  • Eating too much or too little can also interfere with your focus: Too much makes you tired, too little gives you distracting feelings of hunger

    • (If you'd like to learn more refer to: this article or this article.)

    Mood and Emotions
  • Blood sugar levels dropping can make you feel tired and bad tempered.
  • Improving diet increases the "good" gut bacteria, improving nutrient absorption which boots Serotonin to help mediate your moods.
  • A good, balanced diet reduces the degree of inflammation, and may protect against depression.

    • (If you'd like to learn more refer to: this article or this study.)

    What should you eat?

    The mediterranean-style diet is considered a good standard for healthy eating patterns, and nutritious food. It has been attributed to reducing the rate of dementia, and depression. The diet consists of foods high in vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, fish, poultry, eggs, and olive oil.
